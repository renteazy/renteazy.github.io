The problem for property manager

Renting a property is a two-way street. Property managers and landlords need security deposit protection for peace of mind. Yet, security deposits increase barriers to entry for renters.

We’re designing security deposit solutions with property managers, not for property managers.

The problem for renters

Renting a home can be difficult. Renters often face high move-in costs when asked to pay a first month's rent and a security deposit on a potential home.

We believe that renting a home shouldn’t be financially straining and we’re working hard to solve it by removing the need for an upfront security deposit.

Security Deposit Alternative

Security deposit alternatives replace the need to pay high cash deposits when renting a property. This can be paid as a low cost one-time, non-refundable premium.
Instead of paying a high cash security deposit, RentEasy deposit gives renters the option to pay a fraction of the amount, which serves as a guarantee to the property manager that they will be protected in the event that a renter fails to pay rent or damages the property.

  • Free up cash
  • Trusted 3rd party

How it works

  • Potential renters pay a once-off fee for a low-cost surety bond.
  • At lease end, if the renter is liable for damages or missed rent, property managers present RentEasy with a statement of outstanding balances on a property.
  • RentEasy reimburse the property manager within 1 week and recover outstanding balances from the renter.
  • Note: Renters are still liable to RentEasy for amounts owed if rent is missed or the property is damaged.

Benefits for Renters

    Smarter Spending
  • Don’t take out a high-interest loan to pay a security deposit. Pay less to move into a new home and use the money for savings or move-in expenses.
    • Remove hassle
  • Remove the hassle of sourcing and paying a security deposit when moving into a new home and eliminate the headache of trying to get a security deposit back from landlords.
    • Keep your cash
  • Choose how you spend your money today, not a year from now.
